Kabale, Uganda

The Switzerland of Africa

Welcome to Kabale

Tucked into the misty highlands of southwestern Uganda, Kabale is famous for its terraced hillsides, crater lakes, and a cool climate that sets it apart from the rest of the country. It's a place I've long wanted to visit — a quiet escape built into steep green valleys, bordering Rwanda and the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Top Attractions

Lake Bunyonyi

One of Africa's deepest lakes, dotted with 29 islands and free of bilharzia, making it safe for swimming and canoeing.

Terraced Hillsides

Generations of hand-cut terraces climb every hillside, a striking blend of agriculture and landscape art.

Echuya Forest Reserve

A montane forest home to rare birds and primates, and a gateway to nearby Mgahinga Gorilla National Park.
